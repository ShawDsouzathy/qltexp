To understand export business better, let us take a simple calculation. If your manufacturing cost is ₹200 per unit and your export expenses such as packing, shipping, and customs are ₹50 per unit, your total cost becomes ₹250 per unit. If a buyer pays you 5 dollars per unit and the exchange rate is 1 USD = ₹82, then you receive ₹410 per unit. Your profit per unit will be ₹410 minus ₹250 which is ₹160. If you export 1,000 units then your profit becomes ₹160 multiplied by 1,000 which is ₹1,60,000. This shows how exports can multiply your profits compared to selling locally.

Along with profit calculation you should also keep track of important export resources. The DGFT portal helps you with IEC, licenses, and export schemes. The GST portal is used for registration and filing. Customs rules and HS Codes can be found at CBIC. You can file shipping bills and track your exports on ICEGATE. The FIEO website supports exporters with trade fairs and networking. You can also study trade statistics on DGFT Trade Data and get financial help from EXIM Bank. For currency conversion you can use XE Currency Converter. For safe payments you can use SWIFT or PayPal for small orders.
